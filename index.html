About me

I am a 17 year old Mechanical and Computeer Engineering student at Weber State University with primary interests in robotics, software development, 3D modeling, and aerospace. The majority of my work and project experience has involved developing mechanical systems, designing 2D/3D projects, and engineering research.

Over the years I have been part of multiple projects, from which I developed a set of skills in the following programs:

  • SOLIDWORKS
  • AutoCAD  
  • Revit
  • Python
  • MATLAB
  • Arduino
  • Microsoft Office (Excel, Powerpoint, Word)

    • Projects

    Concept Hybrid Rocket Demonstrator (CHRD)

  • Recently, I have been involved in the research of the Concept Hybrid Rocket Demonstrator (CHRD) developed by Dr. Dustin Birch. We created a prototype of the Hybrid Rocket to use it as a testbed for analysis of a variety of rocket nozzle materials
  • In collaboration with the Miller Advanced Research & Solutions Center (MARS), we have been able to amplify the scopes of the project to maximize efficience and data collection from the materials tested

    • Stop and Shoot Vehicle (SSV)

    Spring 2023
  • As part of my freshman design project, I worked with three other students to create a stop and shoot vehicle (SSV). The SSV's objective was to travel over a 25 feet distance, and shoot a small projectile to a target 10 feet away
  • I programmed an Arduino microcontroller to upload commands of different actions to the SSV. During the process, I learned to work with DC and servo motors using an Arduino
  • Also, I created the conceptual design, and virtual simulations of the SSV using SOLIDWORKS

    • Electric Scooter (RUFUS)

  • As a personal project, I created a homemade electric scooter named RUFUS. The primary objective of this project was to create a direct pulley mechanism linking a brushless motor to the back wheel of an old scooter, then create an electric system that enabled me to manually control the speed of the motor using an Arduino microcontroller
  • I modeled specific parts of RUFUS in SOLIDWORKS and 3D printed them. I learned how to work with powerful brushless motors, electronic speed controllers, potentiometers, and the Arduino NANO version

    • Recreational Park (Wooly)

  • Wooly is a personal project in which I modeled and rendered a recreational park inspired on one of my favorite animals, Llamas!. I created it to practice my design skills using the AutoCAD and Revit programs
  • I created many conceptual drafts and technical drawings before making the first drafts in AutoCAD. I subdivided the park in 4 sections that created the shape of a Llama. After I finished the complete AutoCAD model, I rendered a 3D version of it using Revit
